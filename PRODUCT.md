# mhoooo.com Product Brief

## Role

`mhoooo.com` is the credibility and sales surface for Moo's AI automation work. It should help a practical business owner understand what mhoooo builds, see proof through demos, and start with one workflow.

The site is not a futurist portfolio, AI lab manifesto, or generic SaaS landing page.

## Customer

Primary buyer: Thai SME founder/operator who needs fewer manual follow-ups, cleaner customer handling, and faster business execution.

Typical pain:

- Leads arrive through Facebook, Instagram, LINE, email, or WhatsApp and get missed.
- Staff copy/paste the same replies, forms, documents, and follow-ups.
- Business data is scattered across chat, spreadsheets, email, and memory.
- The owner wants automation but does not want to manage a software project.

## Product Promise

Build boring AI systems that do real customer work.

Good examples:

- Reply to new leads and prepare handoff.
- Turn chat threads into follow-up tasks.
- Watch documents, orders, or account activity.
- Produce business dashboards from messy source records.
- Record clear product demos so customers see the workflow before buying.

Bad examples:

- "AI transformation" with no workflow.
- Decorative dashboards with no next action.
- Strategy decks without a working system.
- Generic chatbots that cannot touch the customer's real process.

## Offers

### Demo Sprint

Goal: prove one workflow in a short recorded demo.

Deliverables:

- Workflow map.
- Clickable or screen-recordable prototype.
- One 60-90 second demo.
- Install recommendation.

### Workflow Install

Goal: connect the validated workflow to real customer operations.

Deliverables:

- Integration setup.
- Guardrails and approvals.
- Operator handoff.
- First week support.

### Operating Room

Goal: give the business owner a live control surface for repeat workflows.

Deliverables:

- Shared memory and source records.
- Dashboard or mission board.
- Agent task lanes.
- Monthly maintenance.

## Demo Library Rule

Every public claim needs a demo slot.

Each demo should show:

1. The customer scenario.
2. The raw input.
3. What the system does.
4. The output.
5. The human approval boundary.
6. The time or error saved.

Until a demo is recorded, label it clearly as `recording next`, `internal proof`, or `build proof`. Do not imply shipped customer proof where it does not exist.

## Conversion Path

The site should route customers to a low-friction first step:

`send one workflow -> demo sprint -> install if useful -> monthly care`

No hard sell. The tone is direct, calm, and practical.
