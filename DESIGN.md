# mhoooo.com Design Protocol

## Taste Goal

Make mhoooo.com feel like a competent operator showing working systems, not an AI template promising magic.

Keywords:

- grounded
- operational
- founder-led
- demo-first
- calm
- direct
- Thai SME practical

## Visual Rules

- Use real people, real workflows, product-like surfaces, and demo evidence.
- Prefer tables, ledgers, timelines, status rows, and operator panels over decorative cards.
- Keep corners at `8px` or less.
- Use warm paper, ink, green, rust, and muted blue as the palette. Do not lean on purple/cyan gradients.
- Motion should clarify state, progress, or recording flow. No decorative bounce/glow.
- Typography should feel editorial and operational. Avoid giant marketing claims inside small panels.
- Repeated cards are allowed for offers and demos; avoid cards inside cards.
- The homepage hero must make `mhoooo` visible immediately and explain the offer through supporting copy.

## Copy Rules

Write like a builder selling a useful service:

- "We record the demo first."
- "Start with one workflow."
- "Human approval stays in the loop."
- "If it does not save time or reduce errors, we do not install it."

Avoid:

- "Transform your business."
- "Unlock the power of AI."
- "Seamless end-to-end solution."
- "Revolutionary autonomous agents."
- Vague superlatives.

## Site Structure

1. Hero: brand, boring-product promise, first workflow CTA.
2. Work lanes: what mhoooo actually builds.
3. Demo library: proof queue and recording status.
4. Method: demo sprint to install to maintenance.
5. Founder: why Moo is credible and reachable.
6. Contact: send one workflow.

## Taste Review Checklist

Before shipping a visual change:

- Is the customer work visible within 10 seconds?
- Is every major claim backed by a demo, proof status, or concrete deliverable?
- Did we remove generic AI landing-page tropes?
- Does the page still work if all decorative styling is removed?
- Is the main CTA one workflow, not an abstract consultation?
- Are statuses honest when proof is not recorded yet?
- Does mobile preserve hierarchy without text overflow?

## Demo Frame Rules

Each demo page or clip should include:

- Scenario label.
- Input evidence.
- System action.
- Human approval boundary.
- Output.
- Business result.

The design should make those six parts obvious without needing explanatory prose.
